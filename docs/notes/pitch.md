# ClassPulse — Demo Pitch (~3 min)

## HOOK (15 sec, before touching the screen)

How do we measure learning? Test scores. Which means we find out a lesson didn't work... three weeks after it didn't work. A principal runs a school of 500 students and sees the inside of each classroom maybe twice a semester. Teaching is the only performance profession with no game tape.

We built the game tape. This is **ClassPulse — the heartbeat of the classroom.**

## DEMO (90 sec, screen on)

A teacher records a lesson — any camera, even a phone on a tripod — and uploads it. That's the entire workflow. No sensors, no new hardware in the classroom.

**(Overview screen)** This is what a principal sees: every classroom in the school, with a pulse. Engagement, learning, efficiency — and yes, **fun**. We score fun. You can see the pulse line spike at 14:32 — that's when the teacher cracked a joke. For the first time, "my students love this class" is a data point.

**(Lesson report)** Drill into one lesson. Every score comes with **timestamped evidence** — the AI never says "engagement: 62" without showing you *why*, down to the moment. Attendance took itself. Lesson notes wrote themselves — a searchable record of what was actually taught.

**(Student card — the money shot)** And here's what nobody else has. ClassPulse finds each student in the room, follows them through the whole lesson, and produces this: **Ali's personal video timeline, next to the AI's analysis of Ali.** Engaged for twenty minutes, then the phone came out at 14:05. Sports coaches have had this for decades — player-by-player film review. Teachers never have. Until now.

**(Insights feed)** And because it remembers — built on EverOS agent memory — it sees what no single lesson shows: "Ali's engagement has declined three lessons in a row." That's not a report card. That's an early warning system — you reach a kid *before* he's failing, not after.

## PRIVACY (30 sec — say it BEFORE they ask)

Now — you're all thinking the same word: *surveillance*. So let's be direct, because we designed for this question.

**One:** the video is processed and **deleted**. What's kept is pedagogy — scores, evidence, notes. **Two:** the school owns everything; nothing trains anyone's models. **Three:** there's an **anonymous mode** — full class analytics, zero identities — because engagement statistics don't need names, and schools choose their comfort level. And **four**, the framing that matters most: this data points at *the lesson*, not at kids. It doesn't ask "which student is bad" — it asks "did this teaching *work*, and who needs help." Teachers get a mirror, not a supervisor.

## VISION (20 sec)

Where this goes: post-lesson **highlight reels** for parents — not "your kid was distracted," but "here's the moment your kid nailed the answer." Teacher game-tape reviews, like athletes. A boredom alarm that pings the teacher's watch *during* class: "you lost the back row four minutes ago."

## CLOSE (15 sec)

Ten million classrooms run every day with zero feedback loop. ClassPulse gives every one of them a pulse — built this morning on Butterbase, remembering with EverOS, deployed and live right now at this URL. **Every lesson has a pulse. We think it's time someone took it.**

# Privacy Q&A cheat sheet

- **"Students didn't consent to being filmed."** → Schools already operate cameras and recording policies under existing consent frameworks (FERPA in the US); ClassPulse slots into that policy, it doesn't create it. Plus anonymous mode for districts that want zero identity.
- **"Could this punish students?"** → Scores are diagnostic, not disciplinary — same as a reading assessment. The evidence requirement cuts both ways: no vague accusations, only observable moments a human can review.
- **"Teachers will feel watched."** → The teacher uploads voluntarily and sees their report first. It's a growth tool with an admin view — not an admin tool pointed at teachers. Educator trust is adoption.
- **"Face recognition of minors?"** → Roster matching is opt-in per school, runs at processing time, replaceable by anonymous IDs with one setting. We keep face *matching*, never build a face *database*.
- **"Bias in the AI?"** → Every claim is timestamped and human-verifiable against the clip — auditable by design, which is more than can be said for gut instinct.
