# ClassPulse — Demo Pitch (~3 min)

**Intent (one line, if asked "what does this do?"):** ClassPulse helps teachers become more effective — it keeps the pulse of their class, shows them which kids need their focus and when interest breaks, and gives suggestions. The teacher always stays in the loop: AI observes, the teacher decides.

## HOOK (15 sec, before touching the screen)

A teacher stands in front of 30 kids, teaching and managing the room at the same time. Nobody can watch 30 faces at once — so every day, in every classroom, moments get missed: the kid who quietly gave up in minute 12, the back row that checked out after the exercise. Teaching is the only performance profession with no game tape.

We built the game tape — for the teacher. This is **ClassPulse — the heartbeat of the classroom.**

## DEMO (90 sec, screen on)

A teacher records their lesson — any camera, even a phone on a tripod — and uploads it. That's the entire workflow. No sensors, no new hardware.

**(Overview screen)** This is the teacher's pulse view: engagement, learning, efficiency — and yes, **fun**. We score fun. See the pulse line spike at 14:32? That's when the teacher cracked a joke. And see it drop at 22:10? That's the moment the class lost interest — now the teacher knows exactly *when* their lesson loses people, and can fix the lesson, not guess at it.

**(Lesson report)** Every score comes with **timestamped evidence** — ClassPulse never says "engagement: 62" without showing *why*, down to the moment. Attendance took itself. Lesson notes wrote themselves.

**(Student card — the money shot)** And here's the part built for the question every teacher asks: *"which of my kids needs me?"* ClassPulse follows each student through the lesson and shows their personal timeline: Ali was engaged for twenty minutes — then his interest broke at 14:05, right when the phone came out. Next to it, a **suggestion**: "Ali disengages during long solo exercises — try pairing him up, or a check-in at the 15-minute mark." Not a verdict. A starting point.

**(Insights feed)** And because it remembers — built on EverOS agent memory — it catches what no single lesson shows: "Ali's engagement has declined three lessons in a row." That's an early warning the teacher gets *before* a kid is failing, not after.

**(The line that frames it all)** Notice what ClassPulse never does: it never grades a child, never acts on its own, never replaces the teacher's judgment. **The AI observes; the teacher decides.** Every insight is a suggestion the teacher can accept, dismiss, or correct — and correcting it makes the system smarter. Human in the loop, by design.

## PRIVACY (30 sec — say it BEFORE they ask)

Now — you're all thinking the same word: *surveillance*. So let's be direct, because we designed for this question.

**One — and this is the big one: we don't store the videos.** The video is analyzed, insights are extracted for the teacher, and the video is gone. What remains is pedagogy — scores, evidence, notes — not footage of children. **Two:** the school owns its data; nothing trains anyone's models. **Three:** there's an **anonymous mode** — full class-level insight, zero identities. And **four:** this tool points at *the lesson*, not at kids. It doesn't ask "which student is bad" — it asks "did my teaching work, and who needs my help." It's a mirror for the teacher, not a camera pointed at anyone.

## VISION (20 sec)

Where this goes: a **suggestion coach** that learns each teacher's style — "your class engages 30% more during discussions than lectures." Highlight reels for parents — "here's the moment your kid nailed the answer." And a gentle live nudge on the teacher's watch: "you lost the back row four minutes ago."

## CLOSE (15 sec)

Millions of teachers walk out of class every day asking "did that work?" — and get no answer. ClassPulse answers them: built this morning on Butterbase, remembering with EverOS, live right now at this URL. **Every lesson has a pulse. We help teachers feel it.**

# Privacy Q&A cheat sheet

- **"Do you store video of children?"** → **No. The video is analyzed and discarded — never stored.** Only the derived insights (scores, timestamped notes) remain, and the school owns those.
- **"Students didn't consent to being filmed."** → Schools already operate recording under existing consent frameworks (FERPA in the US); ClassPulse slots into that policy and adds anonymous mode for districts that want zero identity.
- **"Could this punish students?"** → Insights are diagnostic, not disciplinary, and nothing happens without the teacher deciding — human in the loop is a product principle, not a setting.
- **"Teachers will feel watched."** → The teacher records voluntarily and sees their report first — it's their growth tool, like game tape for an athlete. Not a supervision tool pointed at them.
- **"Face recognition of minors?"** → Roster matching is opt-in per school, runs only at analysis time on video that's then discarded, and can be replaced by anonymous IDs with one setting. We never build a face database.
- **"Bias in the AI?"** → Every claim is timestamped and human-verifiable, and the teacher can dismiss or correct any insight — the system is auditable and correctable by design.
